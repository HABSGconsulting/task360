# Task360 – Services Overview and Alignment with Startup Categories

Task360 provides end-to-end compliance, accounting, legal, and fundraising support for startups and growing businesses in India, with a particular focus on e-commerce and online marketplace sellers (Amazon, Flipkart, etc.). Their offerings span three broad phases:

- **Launch** – structuring, incorporation, and registrations.
- **Operate** – accounting, taxation, GST, legal drafting, and ongoing compliance.
- **Grow** – fundraising, valuation, due diligence, financial modelling, M&A, IPO/flip structures.

The tables below summarise their key services, the role each service plays, example startup types that would use them, and how those startups benefit. Startup examples are drawn from the 9 categories in this project: **AI/ML, AgriTech, CleanTech, D2C/E-commerce, FinTech, HealthTech, Manufacturing/DeepTech, SaaS, Others**.

## 1. Launch Phase – Incorporation & Registrations

### 1.1 Incorporation Services

| Service | Role within Service | Example Startup Types | How the Startup Benefits |
|---|---|---|---|
| Private Limited Company Registration | Set up a company with limited liability, distinct legal identity, and better fundraising capability. | SaaS (e.g., SpotDraft, Nirmata), FinTech (e.g., Ambak, Gullak), D2C brand (e.g., Foxtale, Deconstruct) | Suitable legal structure for equity investment, easier cap table management, clearer governance for future fundraising. |
| Limited Liability Partnership (LLP) | Combine partnership flexibility with limited liability and simplified compliance. | Consulting-heavy AI/ML startup, small HealthTech services platform | Lower compliance burden than a PLC, while still providing liability protection for founders. |
| One Person Company (OPC) | Enable a single founder to start a company with limited liability and full management control. | Solo founder running a niche D2C brand, micro SaaS tool | Formal structure without needing multiple directors; good stepping stone before scaling. |
| Foreign Subsidiary Registration | Create an Indian legal entity for an international parent company. | Global SaaS or AI/ML startup entering India, foreign CleanTech company | Compliant Indian presence to hire, invoice, and operate locally while aligning with global structure. |
| Section 8 Company Registration | Incorporate non-profit organisations with legal status and credibility. | Impact-focused AgriTech (e.g., Samunnati) or HealthTech NGO arm, social enterprise | Access to grants and CSR funding, clear non-profit legal identity. |
| Partnership Registration | Formalise business partnerships with legal recognition and clarity on roles. | Small AgriTech services firm, early-stage Manufacturing services partnership | Reduces future disputes by documenting roles and profit-sharing. |
| Sole Proprietorship Registration | Allow individual entrepreneurs to operate businesses with minimal compliance. | Very early-stage D2C seller, small HealthTech practice | Quick start with low compliance, useful before scale and external funding. |

## 5. The Intelligence Dataset Bridge

This profile connects the macro/policy research with Task360's service stack. Each startup in the database can be tagged with:

- **Service cluster relevance** (Launch / Operate / Grow)
- **Specific services likely needed** (e.g., FEMA + ESOP for a Series A FinTech)
- **Engagement hook** (e.g., invite to fundraising clinic, GST workshop, or IPO readiness session)
- **Macro/policy tags** from the research framework (e.g., interest rates, digital infra, GST compliance)

This creates a second, organisation-facing dataset — same startups, different lens — with clear economic value as a relationship intelligence tool for Task360.
